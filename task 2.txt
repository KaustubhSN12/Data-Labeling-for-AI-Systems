How to Review Labels
Now that you're reviewing other people's labels, your job is to think clearly, consistently, and constructively. You'll use the Keep, Fix, or Flag framework to decide whether each set is ready to go or needs a second look. 

 
The Review Framework
Keep - The labels follow the guidelines and make sense. No changes needed. 
Fix - One or more labels are incorrect or inconsistent. You'll explain what should change. 
Flag - Something's unclear. Maybe the message is ambiguous, or the guideline doesn't fully cover it. You'll note what made it hard to decide. 
 
A Few Tips
Focus on intent, tone, and privacy. Are the choices aligned with the definitions you used in Task 1?
Don't be afraid to fix something, just explain why.
Use your best judgment, and be kind. Real people are learning from this, just like you.
 

Let's dive in. You'll review five label sets, one at a time, and apply the Keep/Fix/Flag framework to each. As you go, take note of anything confusing, inconsistent, or missing from the guideline; you'll use those insights to suggest one clear improvement at the end of this exercise.
